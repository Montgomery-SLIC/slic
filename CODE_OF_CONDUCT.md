# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

SLIC is research software developed at the University of Sheffield to support
the study of real-time speech perception. We are committed to maintaining an
open and respectful environment for everyone who uses, contributes to, or
engages with this project - regardless of experience level, academic
background, nationality, or personal identity.

## Expected behaviour

- Be respectful and considerate in all communications.
- Give constructive feedback and accept it graciously.
- Focus criticism on ideas and code, not on people.
- Acknowledge the work of others and give credit where it is due.
- Be mindful that contributors may be students or researchers at varying
  stages of their careers.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, personal attacks, or discriminatory language of any kind.
- Publishing private information (e.g. participant data, email addresses)
  without consent.
- Deliberate misuse of the software in ways that could compromise research
  integrity or participant privacy.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces - including the GitHub
repository, issue tracker, and any other communication channels associated
with this project.
